    Canon Printer Setup : Download, Install & Setup

    Your Canon Printer must be properly configured, installed and connected with desktop computers to serve your printing needs. You must install the designated drivers and software for them to work. Canon printer setup is simple, quick and easy that doesn’t require much technical know-how.

    The most common ways to connect a printer to your PC is through a USB cable, fire wire or ethernet network connections. Some models can be connected wirelessly through Wi-Fi or Bluetooth connections. Hence, before you sit down to set up your Canon Printer; make sure to check connection specifications on your printer package. You can also check the same online.

    Before we move to the actual process, you need to ensure that you have the Installation CD handy for quick and easy setup. You usually get this software CD in your printer package. In case, you have misplaced or lost it, you need not worry about it.

    These drivers are readily available on Canon’s website. All you need to do is download it from the website. You are required to simply look for the drivers meant for your specific printer model. Once you have identified the correct driver, you can easily download it to your system.

    Connecting Your Printer

    First and foremost, make sure to place the printer in such a way that it can be safely connected to both the power source and PC/ Laptop. Now plug in your printer’s power cable to the power source and switch it on. Press the printer’s power button which is located either on top or front panel. It can be easily identified as it differs from other control buttons and duly marked with power icon. You should hear the printer’s page feed mechanism and the printer will light up. As your printer is powered up, simultaneously connect your PC to a power source, turn it on. You must make sure that both the printer and PC are properly connected to the power socket and turned on.

    Once the PC is up and running, you need to connect the printer to it. Your printer is usually accompanied by two cables. One is the power cable which is easily recognized by the power plug on one end. The other cable, most often, will be a USB cable. This cable should effortlessly fit into your PC’s USB port. It could be either on the side of your laptop computer or on the front or back of the CPU box.

    Make sure that you correctly identify the computer’s USB port. Look closely for the cable connector and port’s design. The cable must perfectly fit in the port.You are required to connect the non-USB end of the cable to the printer. Once, you have ensured that printer and computer are properly and securely connected and turned on; we can now proceed further.
